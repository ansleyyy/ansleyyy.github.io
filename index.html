Eating disorders

"Eating disorders are serious mental illnesses, NOT a lifestyle choice."













Types of Eating Disorders




Anorexia Nervosa

People generally view themselves as overweight, even if they're dangerously underweight. They tend to constantly monitor their weight, avoid eating certain types of foods, and severely restrict their calorie intake.

Bulimia Nervosa

Patients of Bulimia Nervosa experience a viscious cycle of binging and purging. During a binge, the person usually feels thay they cannot stop eating or control how much they are eating. Then they attempt to purge through methods like force vomitting, fasting and excessive exercise to compensate for the calories consumed and relieve gut discomfort.

Binge Eating

They typically eat unusually large amounts of food in relatively short periods of time and feel a lack of control during binges. Additionally, they do not restrict calories or use purging behaviors to compensate for their binges
